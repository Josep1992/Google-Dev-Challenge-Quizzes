Orcas

Orcas are apex predators, at the top of the food chain. No other animals (except for humans) hunt orcas.

  • Scientific Name:Orcinus orca
  • Average Length: Males - 32 feet.
  • Average Lifespan: Male:17yrs - Female: 29yrs.
  • Habitat: Killer whales range from the Bering Sea to Southern California.

An orca is a marine mammal. Did you know that orcas are actually dolphins? In fact, they are the largest member of the dolphin family! Dolphins and whales are closely related – both are in the order Cetacea, which includes whales, dolphins, and porpoises. Orcas were given the name “killer whale” by ancient sailors’ observations of groups of orcas hunting and preying on larger whale species.
